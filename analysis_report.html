Interpretation

The scatter plot above shows the relationship between health spending per person and life expectancy across countries for the year 2010. The Pearson correlation coefficient (r) is 0.562, indicating a moderate positive correlation between these variables.

This suggests that countries with higher health spending per person tend to have higher life expectancy. However, it’s important to note that this relationship is not perfect, and other factors such as healthcare system efficiency, public health measures, and social determinants of health also play significant roles in determining life expectancy.
